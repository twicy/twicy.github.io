Hi! My name is **Yuchen Tang**. I am currently a **Member of Technical Staff on the Kernel Team**, where I work on the high-performance **FlashBlade and FlashArray storage systems**. I'm excited to contribute to the low-level technologies that power modern storage infrastructure and help deliver performance and reliability at scale.

I recently completed my **M.S. in Computer Engineering at Stony Brook University**. During my time there, I had the opportunity to work closely with [Prof. Michael Ferdman](https://compas.cs.stonybrook.edu/~mferdman/) and [Prof. Erez Zadok](https://www3.cs.stonybrook.edu/~ezk/) on several systems research projects, including **high-performance Ethernet device drivers** and **Compute Express Link (CXL)–based zero-copy VM live migration**. I also worked on a CUDA project with [Prof. Mikhail Dorojevets](https://www.ece.stonybrook.edu/~midor/), optimizing an SGEMM kernel to reach **80–85% of cuBLAS performance**.

Before graduate school, I worked for **two years at Huawei as an Operating System Engineer**, where I conducted research on **tiered memory systems** (industry demonstration) and **HPC systems** (award-winning work).

I received my **B.S. in Computer Science from the University of Michigan** and **B.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Shanghai Jiao Tong University**.
